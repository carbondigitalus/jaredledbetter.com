I joined the world of entrepreneurship in Okinawa, Japan in mid 2012.

This is an interesting story. I was working as a government contractor when one of my co-workers recieved a 10-day notice that his employment was over. During those days he had to pack up his life, cancel all of his utilities and phone, and sell his vehicles. This was a completely unrealistic demand from our employer, but I helped him meet the deadline. It was this that led to my decisions.

The Japanese people are known to give gifts as a way of saying thank you. Since my wife was Japanese, and my Brazilian Jiu Jitsu (BJJ) Professor was half-Japanese, I felt that giving him a gift would be a nice gesture. Especially since I knew that my employment was temporary and that I may not remain in Japan.

I decided that a BJJ Gi (Uniform) would be the best gift ever! I spent the next few months researching the multitude of possibilities in creating a uniform. The various weave types, weights of the weave, and so much more. I established connections with several manufacturers overseas to produce a product. From there the last piece was to create a mockup. I was fortunate enough to acquire a copy of PhotoShop and began designing my first BJJ Gi. It took me a month or so to work out the details of the product, get an order ready and placed. About a month later, I had a product in hand. With great excitement, I presented the product to my Professor. Afterwards, I felt a void. I had all of this knowledge and no outlet. Alas, my first business was formed.
